The King in Yellow contains ten stories. The first four are linked by the theme of the forbidden play; the remaining six are unrelated romances set in Paris.

## The Four Play-Linked Stories

### The Repairer of Reputations

Set in a future New York (1920), this story follows Hildred Castaigne, a man who believes he is destined to become king after reading the play. The story explores themes of delusion, aristocratic decay, and the seductive power of the King in Yellow.

### The Mask

A scientist uses a mysterious formula created by a Japanese colleague to reveal the true souls of people. The story is a meditation on identity, art, and the hidden nature of reality.

### The Court of the Dragon

A man is followed by a sinister church organist who seems to know his darkest secrets. When he confronts the organist, he is transported to the court of the King in Yellow. A powerful story about guilt, surveillance, and damnation.

### The Yellow Sign

A church watchman and an artist become obsessed with a mysterious symbol carved into a window. The Yellow Sign appears at their doorstep, and a hideous figure comes to claim them. The most famous Chambers story and the purest expression of the King in Yellow mythos.

## The Six Romance Stories

### The Demoiselle d'Ys

A man in Brittany stumbles into a time portal and meets a woman from the 15th century. A romantic fantasy with a tragic ending.

### The Prophets' Paradise

A series of prose poems about a group of artists living in Paris. Experimental and lyrical.

### The Street of the Four Winds

A story about a struggling artist and a mysterious cat. A gentle romance in the Latin Quarter.

### The Street of the First Shell

A wartime romance set during the siege of Paris. The most realistic story in the collection.

### The Street of Our Lady of the Fields

A love story about an American artist in Paris. Captures the bohemian atmosphere of the Latin Quarter.

### Rue Barrée

A short romance set in a Parisian boarding house. Light and charming.
