## Places

**Carcosa** — The ancient, forbidden city that serves as the setting for the play *The King in Yellow*. Described as having "black stars" in its sky and "twin suns" that sink into the Lake of Hali. Its exact location is unknown; it exists in another dimension or time. Carcosa has become synonymous with cosmic horror and unknowable dread.

**The Lake of Hali** — A vast, still body of water near Carcosa. In the play, twin suns sink into this lake. The Lake of Hali represents the boundary between our world and the realm of the King.

**Yhtill** — A city mentioned in the play, possibly a sister city to Carcosa or the last bastion against the King's influence.

**Aldebaran** — A star (alpha Tauri) associated with the Hyades cluster. In Chambers' mythos, Aldebaran is linked to the King in Yellow. The Hyades form a V-shaped star cluster that Chambers associates with the horns of the Yellow Sign.

**The Hyades** — A star cluster that Chambers connects to the Yellow Sign. The V-shape of the Hyades is said to form the horns of the sign.

## Characters

**The King in Yellow** — The mysterious ruler of Carcosa. His true nature is never revealed. He is both a character in the play and a cosmic entity. The play itself is named after him, and reading it is said to bring irreversible madness.

**Cassilda** — A character in the play, known for the famous line: "Strange is the night where black stars rise, and strange moons circle through the skies. But stranger still is lost Carcosa."

**Camilla** — Another character in the play. The relationship between Cassilda and Camilla is ambiguous and haunting.

**The Stranger** — A mysterious figure who appears in the play, possibly an avatar of the King himself.

**The Phantom of Truth** — A character who guides others toward terrible revelations. Appears in the play as a messenger of doom.

**Hastur** — A name of uncertain origin. In Chambers, Hastur is mentioned as a place or perhaps a being. Later expanded by H.P. Lovecraft and August Derleth into a Great Old One.

## Concepts

**The Yellow Sign** — A symbol of the King in Yellow. Those who see it are marked for doom. It is described as a "Yellow Sign" that appears in various contexts — on a torn page, carved into a window, or drawn in the air. The sign resembles a V-shape (like the Hyades star cluster) combined with other geometric elements.

**The Play** — A two-act drama titled *The King in Yellow*. Reading it is fatal: those who read the first act become obsessed, and those who read the second act lose their sanity or die. The play is described as beautiful and terrible, with a haunting power.

**The Lost City** — Carcosa, the city of the King in Yellow, lost to time and memory. Its location is unknown and possibly unreachable.

**Black Stars** — In Carcosa's sky, the stars are black against a pale sky. This inverted cosmology is one of the most haunting images in weird fiction.
